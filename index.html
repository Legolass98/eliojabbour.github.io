About Me

I am a Robotics Engineer specializing in Human-Robot Interaction (HRI). My PhD work at Inria focuses on "MPC-B", a predictive shared control framework that safely blends human inputs with autonomous assistance. I solve problems where robots must be compliant collaborators, not just automated machines.

Interactive Demo: Linear vs. MPC

Experience the difference between standard Linear Blending and my MPC-B framework.
Goal: Drag the "Human Input" (Blue Dot) to move the Robot (Red Dot) towards the Target (Green). Try to push the robot into the walls!

Safety Status: Safe

What is happening?

  • Linear Blending: The robot naively averages your input with the assistance. If you push towards a wall, it might crash (Constraint Violation). It reacts late and moves jerkily.
  • MPC-B (My Thesis): The robot predicts future collisions. Even if you push hard towards the wall, it smoothly decelerates and stops exactly at the limit. It optimizes for safety and smoothness in real-time.
